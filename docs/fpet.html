Family Planning Estimation Tool (FPET)

Summary

The Family Planning Estimation Tool (FPET) was designed to produce annual estimates and forecasts of family planning indicators such as modern contraceptive use (mCPR), unmet need, and demand satisfied by modern methods. FPET considers survey data, service statistics (where determined to be of adequate quality), and regional and global historical patterns of change to produce annual estimates beyond the last survey and into the future.

The current modeling approach is described in Alkema et al. (2024). This approach builds off collaborative research with the United Nations Population Division (UNPD). References to earlier and related work are included below.

This work was supported, in whole or in part, by the Gates Foundation (INV-00844).

Software

The model is written in R and Stan. The R package is available at https://github.com/AlkemaLab/fpet2 and described at https://alkemalab.github.io/fpet2/. For any questions, feedback or suggestions, please contact Leontine at lalkema(at)umass.edu.

An online app by the Track20 project is available at https://fpet.track20.org/.
